Project 1:
In the process of branding Nathalie's artworks, I've been utilizing Adobe apps extensively, particularly Adobe Illustrator for designing logos and stylescapes. However, during my free time, I've also been experimenting with Photoshop and Premiere Pro.

In the first project, my concept for the client centered around creating a stamp-like image featuring a blossoming pink flower, which was a specific request from the client. Before I started to work on the design I was searching for inspiration (for instance, this is how I came across the stamp idea and the vivid colors). In the beginning, I was experimenting with two designs, but in the end, I stuck with a cherry blossom on a stamp. Anyway, I did try out different colors to have some options. For selecting colours Canva's color palettes proved to be incredibly helpful as they offer a wide range of options. In the end, my group used my idea with the stamp but we used a logo that my groupmate made, so I merged the two into one.
However, I had the idea of making flyers for the client as an offline media tool, therefore I could use my previous work with a little twist (a short slogan).

Project X:
My final project's main product was a podcast recording, featuring the topic of Gen-z and two guests. The way I recorded the sounds wasn't that complicated since I asked a teacher to show me how the gadgets, and microphone work, but preparing everything else was a bigger challenge. While the recording process was pretty manageable, thanks to guidance from a Teacher who explained the use of the necessary audio equipment, the overall preparation had some significant challenges. I got the advice that I should think up a detailed plan to share with the guests, outlining the structure of the podcast, so I started with that. This preparation also included a list for myself where I highlighted the key thoughts and questions that I wanted to share and ask to ensure a seamless flow of conversation, even during potential pauses. About the post-recording, the editing process was carried out using Premiere Pro. I mainly just put together the pre-recorded parts and the main conversation, which fortunately required minimal editing due to the natural and fluent dialogue. To add a nice touch, I created a custom background for the podcast. This involved designing a collage using Adobe Illustrator, providing a visually appealing background for the final product.
For those interested in the creative and technical aspects of this project, including the development of the background wallpaper and the podcast script, further details can be found in the accompanying document.

For Fontys:
As an additional task, I made a video for Fontys, about a day of my life.
My task was to make a short (max 2 minutes) video, about what a school day of mine looks like, with a morning routine, introduction, and afternoon activities.
Overall my idea was to take the clips on a fun day when more things happen to me, this is why I chose the one when my group had to go to Breda to present our project.
I think the hardest part of this assignment was not forgetting about taking the clips throughout my day and also speaking in front of the camera by myself. However, I think in the end, I had enough clips so everything turned out well.
Editing the short film, was alright since I already had some knowledge of the use of Premier Pro. I tried to add a nice touch to it with some music in the background, and with some text under the parts when the user might be lost what is going on.
In the end, I got great feedback about the video, so I think I managed this short project in a great way.
