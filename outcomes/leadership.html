Project X:
For my final project, I decided to make a podcast as my main product, to make this podcast interesting not just that I have to carry out great research, but I also had to invite the right people for a fascinating talk. One of the guests that I decided to invite was a Fontys Teacher and a book writer who has previous knowledge of the chosen topic. I knew he probably didn't have a lot of time, so I tried to reach him as soon as possible, so we could find a timeline which is good for everyone. In the end, he chose a date that limited my time to finish everything before the recording day. I had to keep very focused and arrange everything in two weeks. It was a great exercise in multitasking. Since the studio wasn't reserved at that time, I had to figure out how the audio lab worked, write several emails to gather this information, and ensure the fixed date was suitable for the other guest. Additionally, I created and sent a survey to at least forty people, completed my library study, and kept my project coach updated.
All in all, I truly learned a few things about management, which I believe is a very useful skill to acquire. I think it could be beneficial for my future projects (and in the work field as well).
